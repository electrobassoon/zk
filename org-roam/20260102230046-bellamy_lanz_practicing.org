How to Practice: Frameworks from Atheltic Training

Music Performance is an athletic pursuit
- Physical Demands
  - Endurance
  - Strength
  - Speed
  - Coordination
  - Risks of injuries
- Long Term Planning
  - Goals
  - Concerts
  - Auditions
  - Degrees
- Event Specific Skills
  - Double Tonguing
  - Tapers to niente
  - Double tongue at 140 on D from Figaro
  - Rite of Spring in tune from nothing

Session Types
- Speed/Power -- about 50%
- Cross Train: complimentary skills, muscles, or systems -- about 10%
- Active Recovery: stretch, shake out -- about 10%
- Endurance: exhaustion or simulate performance -- about 25%
- Rest -- about 5%

Endurance: 3-4 per week, not running pieces half the time
- Long tones
- Reed making
- Slow etudes
- run through / reps
- Double tonguing
- Standing
- Anything to exhaustion

Speed/Power: 5-6 per week
- Scales (fast)
- "sprints" small unit getting faster and faster
- modifier - jumping jacks or trampoline before techincal passages, metronome work
- long tones
- tonguing drills
- vibrato exercises
- targeted scales/etudes

Cross train: 1-2 per week
- Sight reading
- Improv
- Score study
- Reference listening
- Contextual research

Active Recovery: 1-2 per week
- fun run
- jam session
- arrangement/transcription/composition
- discovery listening

Prehab/Rehab: daily
- reed making
- stretching
- breath work

Race Specific: as needed near event
- Dress rehearsal
- Run throughs
- Mock audition
